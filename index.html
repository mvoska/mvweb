ABOUT ME

About Me

I'm an undergrad at Northeastern University in Boston studying computer engineering and entrepreneurship. I'm CEO & Co-Founder of Flytenow, a ride sharing platform for pilots and enthusiasts. At Northeastern I'm involved with the NU Entrepreneurs Club where I directed the Husky Startup Challenge. Off campus, I try to keep up with all the workshops and startup events in Boston. Overall, I'm trying to advance aviation through new technologies, making it not only accessible to everyone, but making aviation a viable means of everyday transportation.

Prior Ventures

I started my first company, Voska Electronics, in 2005, servicing my town with affordable IT support that they could trust. In 2011, I co-founded GigaStorm: a mobile application and website development firm with Aakash Patel and Andrew Mass, we've since taken our skills learned at GigaStorm and are applying them to Flytenow with Alan Guichard as of August 2013.

WHAT I DO

Entrepreneurship

I have been involved with the NU Entrepreneurs Club since Fall 2012 and I'm working with the Sherman Center for Entrepreneurship, building out the NU makerspace for engineering entrepreneurs. I love startups and I'm always up for bouncing ideas off one another over some burritos.

Computer Engineering

At Northeastern University, I am a computer engineering major and a tech enthusiast. I closely follow the tech market and am fascinated by new hardware and software breakthroughs.

Fly Planes

I obtained my private pilot license (Airplane Single Engine Land) in September of 2011 and have logged 85+ hours since, flying C-152, C-172, PA-28, and an EA300L at 5K6 and BED.

Leadership Development

The ability to persuade and motivate people has alway sparked my interest and I strive to improve my leadership through Civil Air Patrol, Air Force ROTC, and Silver Wings.
